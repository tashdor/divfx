## Clarifications — original text is correct

Items here were flagged in an earlier draft of this page as errors. Checking them against
primary sources showed the handbook was right and the correction was wrong. They are kept
because the original wording is terse enough to be worth expanding, not because it is mistaken.

### DCI X'Y'Z' encoding

> **In [Color Spaces](color.md#dci-xyz):** "implicitly encoded as gamma 2.6 with an additional
> compensation factor to increase the range of producible white points."

**This is correct, including the stated reason.** An earlier revision of this page claimed the
"white points" rationale was wrong and that the constant was really about luminance headroom.
That claim was itself wrong, and is retracted.

The encoding, per SMPTE ST 428-1 and reproduced in *Color and Mastering for Digital Cinema*
(Ch. 4, "Transfer Function"), is:

```
CV(X') = INT[ 4095 × (X / 52.37)^(1/2.6) ]
```

and likewise for Y' and Z', with XYZ as tristimulus values above black in cd/m².

The question is why the normalizing constant is 52.37 rather than the 48 cd/m² reference white
that SMPTE D-Cinema specifies. *Color and Mastering for Digital Cinema* answers it directly:

!!! quote
    "The reason for the use of the 52.37 is that the gamut of the encoding space is increased and
    in particular there are more white point color temperatures along the CIE D-Illuminant line on
    a chromaticity diagram that can be encoded at the maximum luminance, 48.00 cd/m²."

    "In order to provide headroom for possible changes in white point preference, the
    normalization factor was changed to allow the encoding of D65 at the maximum luminance of
    48 cd/m² … the D65 point is the point that forces the 52.37 constant because the D55 and the
    Equal Energy points are well inside the encoded gamut."

That is precisely "to increase the range of producible white points."

The mechanism is worth spelling out, because it is the part the original sentence leaves implicit.
Normalize by 48 and the encoding white point — equal, maximum code values [4095 4095 4095] —
lands at XYZ = [48 48 48], chromaticity x = y = 1/3. On the 48 cd/m² plane that gamut encloses
D55, the Equal Energy point, and the DCI reference projector white point, **but not D61 or D65**.
D65 fails because its Z tristimulus at Y = 48 exceeds 48:

```
Z(D65) at Y = 48  =  48 × (1 − 0.3127 − 0.3290) / 0.3290  ≈  52.3
```

Choosing 52.37 pulls D65 inside the encodable set. The often-quoted consequence — that Y' is
capped at code value 3960 while X' and Z' may reach 4095 — follows *from* that choice rather than
causing it. As the same text notes, X' and Z' are allowed the higher range precisely "because they
carry no luminance information."

**Done:** the D65 derivation and the Equal Energy white-point distinction are now folded into
[Color Spaces and Transfer Functions](color.md#dci-xyz). This entry is retained only as a record of
the retraction (see [What this changed](#what-this-changed)).

Additional items for a v1.1 camera section:

- **LogC4 / ARRI Wide Gamut 4**, introduced with the ALEXA 35 (2022, ALEV 4 sensor, 17 stops).
  LogC4 is EI-independent, with mid-gray at roughly 28% code value (1140/4095) versus LogC3's
  ~39%. AWG4 is larger than Rec.2020 but sits fully inside ACES AP0 to limit virtual colors. Part
  of ARRI REVEAL color science. LogC3/AWG3 remains correct for ALEXA Mini, AMIRA, and
  ALEXA LF/Mini LF.
- **ARRICORE**, announced 31 July 2025 with the ALEXA 35 Xtreme — an RGB (not Bayer) codec at
  roughly half ARRIRAW's data rate, with sensitivity, white balance, and tint still not baked in.

**House style:** the company brands itself **ARRI** (all caps), with products styled ALEXA,
ARRIRAW, AMIRA, ARRICORE. The 2017 text uses "Arri" and "Alexa" throughout. This port has
normalized to ARRI/ALEXA; confirm that matches your preference.

### Codecs

- **ProRes RAW** (2018) postdates this edition and should be covered. In 2026 it is recorded
  in-camera and via Atomos recorders; Windows support requires Apple's separate decoder
  package.
- **OpenEXR** is now at 3.4.x. Changes worth citing since 2017: the OpenEXRCore C API (3.1) for
  thread-safe non-blocking access; DWAA/DWAB lossy compression; HTJ2K lossless compression
  (3.4); ID Manifest attributes (3.0); and new standard camera/lens/production metadata
  attributes including `colorInteropID` (3.2). Note that the **ACES container (SMPTE ST 2065-4) is
  a constrained subset of EXR** — ST 2065-4:2023 §8.19 requires `compression = 0` (uncompressed),
  in both the 2013 and 2023 editions. But that rule is specifically about the ST 2065-4 container,
  which §1 scopes to **ACES2065-1 (AP0)** interchange/archival images (its `chromaticities`
  attribute is fixed to AP0, so ACEScg can't live in one). ACEScg working files, VFX plates, and
  renders are ordinary EXRs and compress normally (ZIP/PIZ/DWA) — they are not ST 2065-4
  containers. See [Drafts → ACES](aces.md#container) for the full note.
- **Avid DNx**: at IBC 2025 Avid rebranded the family to simply "Avid DNx", dropping the
  HD/HR/GX suffixes in favor of the five quality levels. The 2025 revision of SMPTE ST 2019-1
  (VC-3) folded most of DNx GX into the core spec.
- The Apple ProRes white paper URL cited in the original still resolves.

### Rec.2020 display coverage

The 2017 claim that few displays can reproduce Rec.2020 is **still substantially accurate** — no
consumer or reference display covers the full gamut (its primaries are monochromatic), and
high-end 2025–26 OLED and QD-OLED panels measure roughly 75–85% BT.2020 while hitting ~97–99%
DCI-P3, per public measurement (RTINGS). Coverage is measurement-space-dependent (higher in CIE
1931 *xy* than CIE 1976 *u′v′*). See [Drafts → HDR](hdr.md#mastering-in-practice) for the
cited figures.

What the passage lacks is the practical nuance: Rec.2020 functions as a **container** gamut. HDR
is mastered in **P3-D65 limited inside a Rec.2100/Rec.2020 encoding**, not graded to true
Rec.2020 primaries.

### Univisium and 2.00:1

The [Univisium reference](resolutions.md#high-definition) is accurate — 2.00:1 is indeed
Storaro's advocated format, and *Jurassic World* (2015) was shot and released 2.00:1 (the DP
wanted 2.40, Spielberg pushed for 1.85 headroom for the dinosaurs, and they compromised at
2.00:1 inside a 1.85 DCP container).

What needs updating is the framing: 2.00:1 is now mainstream across streaming originals, not a
recent indie curiosity.

### What this changed

One published claim was **wrong and has been retracted** — see
[DCI X'Y'Z' encoding](#dci-xyz-encoding). It was listed as a factual error in the handbook when
the handbook was in fact correct, and the replacement wording I proposed misattributed the
purpose of the 52.37 constant. That is the failure mode worth guarding against in this kind of
review: a plausible-sounding derivation that is arithmetically true (52.37 ≈ 48 × (4095/3960)^2.6
does hold) but causally backwards. The code-value ceiling of 3960 is a *consequence* of choosing
52.37 to enclose D65, not the reason for it.

Claims marked web-only are not necessarily wrong — most are well-supported by current
manufacturer and standards-body sources. They simply have not been checked against a primary
document, and should be before anything goes to print.
